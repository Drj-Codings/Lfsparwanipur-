#Rules to be followed in school.

GENERAL RULES

  1. Continuance on the rolls of the school is subject to the satisfactory progress in the studies. Any student who fails for the second consecutive year in the same class will not be permitted to continue in the school.
  2. Student must be regular and punctual in the class. Leave of absence during the term will not be granted except under very special circumstances. For Leave of absence prior sanction is to be obtained from the principal in writing.
  3. Only a minimum of 85% of attendance of the working days will qualify a student for promotion.
  4. Request for leave on medical grounds must be supported by a certificate from a registered medical practitioner. In this case, the application must be forwarded, at least on the second day of the leave.
  5. Fees must be paid regularly. A student's name is liable to be struck off, if fees are in arrears, at the end of the respective quarters. All dues and arrears must be cleared before the half yearly and annual examinations respectively.
  6. Students may be required to attend extra classes on any working day after school hours or on holidays, provided they have been informed on the previous day.
  7. Students shall not be allowed to leave the school during the school hours, so parents are advised not to come with such requests.
  8. Students shall not be permitted to go for any other programs or functions during the school hours.
  9. All the students have to take part in the extra and co-curricular activities and physical training for which no exemption shall be granted.
  10. Students are strictly prohibited from bringing anything to school other than the study materials and their food. They are forbidden to bring costly articles to school. The school cannot be responsible for the articles lost.
  11. Gifts or donations to the teachers are not allowed.
  12. Students are not allowed to meet their parents or visitors during school hours.
  13. The School Leaving Certificate shall be given only to those students who have fully cleared their dues and produced a written application from the parent or guardian.
  14. Irregular attendance, habitual want of sincerity and commitment, disobedience and any kind of cheating or serious misbehaviours even outside the school shall be viewed seriously.

SCHOOL FEE

  1. Fees are charged for the whole Academic year. The tuition fee is remitted monthly, that is, in twelve installments.
  2. The first installment of the fees shall be paid at the time of admission.
  3. Fees are to be deposited as per the dates mentioned in the fee record.
  4. Defaulters shall remit the fee with a fine of Rs. 25/- on the days given on the fee-card.
  5. A student's name is liable to be struck off, if the fees are in arrears at the end of the respective term.

RECOMMENDATIONS TO THE PARENTS

  1. Parents are welcome to meet the principal to discuss on the progress of their children with prior appointment.
  2. Parents are requested to co-operate with the school authorities by ensuring that the children observe active part in the activities of the school.
  3. Private tuition is not encouraged; the parents are requested to give personal attention to the children's studies and help them in their doubts as far as possible.
  4. Parents should check the school calendar/diary regularly and sign the reports recorded by the teachers and the assignments and the home-work given.punctuality and discipline, prepare their lessons and take.
  5. Parents are not allowed to meet the teachers in the classroom or in the staff room without the permission of the principal. Those who wish to seek information or any suggestion may do so during the parents-teachers' meetings. Those who wish to make any complaint about the education of their children or any other matters that concern the running of the school should do so directly to the principal and not to the teachers.
  6. When a student is likely to be absent, owing to illness for a long period, the principal must be informed, through an application for leave, supported by a medical certificate within a week. Failure to do so will result in the child's name being removed from the school roll.
  7. The principal or a teacher may punish a student for habitual idleness, disobedience, misconduct, indiscipline, failure in preparing lessons etc. by suspension or removal.
  8. The school authority is not responsible for the safety of the students after the school hours. Necessary arrangements should be made to take the children home immediately after the class.
  9. No school business shall be transacted on Saturdays and holidays or outside the school hours. No school business shall be carried out in the residence of the Principal or of the staff.
  10. Children must be trained in self-help by encouraging them to work and study. They should be taught to keep their rooms neat and tidy, make their own beds, polish their shoes, carry their own bags etc. Formation of such habits in the early life will inculcate in them the dignity of work, a fundamental necessity for a successful career.
  11. Meeting students during the school hours in the school is discouraged. However, in case of emergency, the person concerned could do it with the permission of the principal.

INSTRUCTIONS TO STUDENTS

  1. In your dress and behaviour, keep a sense of self-respect.Right behaviour is the expression of a fine character and good personality.
  2. Be simple in your ways and words.
  3. English is the medium of communication in the school. No other language is allowed in the school campus.
  4. Avoid using indecent language.
  5. Courtesy begets courtesy. Make use of the word 'PLEASE' and 'THANK YOU' often. Try to make only constructive criticism.
  6. Be polite and obliging in all your dealings with others. Always try to be a good companion to everyone, helping and supporting them.
  7. Respect your elders and teachers, stand up whenever you see them and greet them as a gesture of respect and love.
  8. Be polite and helpful to the strangers coming to the school.
  9. Before and after the class, stand up for a minute and pray silently.
  10. No exemption shall be given, from the school programs and functions.assembly.in full uniform, including the footwear.
  11. Every student is expected to be present for the daily.
  12. It is strictly compulsory for every student to come to school.
  13. The permission of the principal is required for any students' activities such as found-raising, ticket selling, parties, class organizations etc.
  14. Forging parent's signature or altering marks/grades or ranks will be viewed seriously.

SCHOOL LIBRARY

  1. The school library is equipped with suitable books, stocked to facilitate healthy reading. Students are exhorted to make use of the Library and Reading room. Books and periodicals must be carefully handled and kept neat and tidy.
  2. Books shall be issued to the students for reading, during their Library periods. They shall return these books before leaving the Library.
  3. Students shall not take books home without the permission of the Librarian. Books shall be returned in time. A fine of Rs. 5/- per day will be charged from the defaulters.
  4. The loss, disfigurements, scratching, scribbling, drawing or tearing of books shall be compensated for, by the person to whom the books have been issued, with the full cost of the books.
  5. Students shall make use of the Library books, but misuse will result in the loss of the right to get books from the Library.

SCHOOL LABORATORY

  1. Laboratory equipments are expensive and they have to be handled carefully. When equipments are given to the students for experiments, they must be handed over back to the teachers in-charge before leaving the Laboratory.
  2. Students shall not remove the Lab items without the permission of the in-charge.
  3. In case of loss discovered but not owned up, for willful breakages or serious damages, the individual students of the groups will have to meet the expenses of repairs or replacements.
  4. No chemicals may be taken out of the Laboratory.
  5. Students shall enrich the Laboratory with their own keen interest, donating useful items of their experience to be kept in the Laboratory.

EXAMINATIONS AND PROMOTIONS

  1. The annual promotions are decided through a system of continuous assessment throughout the year. Mark/Grade obtained in the annual examinations alone shall not determine the promotion of the students. The performance in the Unit Tests, Terminal Examinations and Regular Class Works also shall be considered for annual promotions.
  2. If a student does not show steady and consistent progress in studies, he/she is not liable to be promoted.
  3. There shall be no re-examination or examination out of schedule.
  4. For being promoted, a student must secure a pass grade (a minimum of 40% marks in every subject and 50% in aggregate).
  5. No leave except on medical ground shall be sanctioned during the examinations. Medical leave is to be applied for, when the leave is taken, supported by a medical certificate. However, it is not a substitute for examination. The overall performance of the student shall be considered in the regard.
  6. Students detected in giving orobtaining or attempting to give or obtain unfair assistance at the examination shall be disqualified.
  7. A student who is unsuccessful in the same class for two consecutive years will automatically be disqualified from continuance of study in the school.
